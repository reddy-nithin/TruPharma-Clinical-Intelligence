Morphine is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 0.3 nM, showing 240-fold selectivity over kappa. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 2.5 hours
Metabolism: Morphine is 90% metabolized by glucuronidation by UGT2B7 and sulfation at positions 3 and 6. Morphine can also be metabolized to codeine, normorphine, and morphine ethereal sulfate.
