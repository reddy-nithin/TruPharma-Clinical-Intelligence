PHARMACOLOGY: HYDROMORPHONE

Identifiers: RxCUI=3423, ChEMBL=CHEMBL398707, PubChem CID=5284570
Molecular: C17H19NO3, MW=285.34

Receptor Binding Affinities:
  - mu: Ki = 0.28 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - kappa: Ki = 2.8 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - delta: Ki = 38.0 nM (source: ChEMBL)

Potency vs morphine: 1.14x

Hydromorphone is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 0.3 nM, with moderate selectivity over kappa (10.0-fold). Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 2.5 hours
Metabolism: The metabolism of hydromorphone is mainly hepatic and it is represented by the generation of hydromorphone-3-glucuronide through glucuronidation reactions.
