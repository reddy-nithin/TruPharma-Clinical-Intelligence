PHARMACOLOGY: NALOXONE

Identifiers: RxCUI=7242, ChEMBL=CHEMBL80, PubChem CID=5284596
Molecular: C19H21NO4, MW=327.4

Receptor Binding Affinities:
  - mu: Ki = 1.0 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - kappa: Ki = 15.85 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - delta: Ki = 15.85 nM (source: ChEMBL)

Potency vs morphine: 0.32x

Naloxone is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 1.0 nM, showing 16-fold selectivity over kappa. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 2.25 hours
Metabolism: Naloxone primarily undergoes glucuronidation to form naloxone-3-glucuronide. Naloxone is also N-dealkylated to noroxymorphone or undergoes 6-keto reduction to naloxol.
  Danger level: Unknown
