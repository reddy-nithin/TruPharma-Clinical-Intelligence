PHARMACOLOGY: HYDROCODONE

Identifiers: RxCUI=5489, ChEMBL=CHEMBL1457, PubChem CID=5284569
Molecular: C18H21NO3, MW=299.4

Receptor Binding Affinities:
  - mu: Ki = 9.5 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - kappa: Ki = 260.0 nM (source: ChEMBL)

Potency vs morphine: 0.03x

Hydrocodone is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 9.5 nM, showing 27-fold selectivity over kappa. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 8.0 hours
Metabolism: Hydrocodone undergoes oxidative O-demethylation to form [hydromorphone], a more potent active metabolite. Though hydromorphone is active it is not present in sufficient quantities to contribute significantly to hydrocodone's therapeutic effects. Both hydrocodone and hydromorphone form 6-α- and 6-β-h

Toxicology:
  LD50 (rat, oral): 150.0 mg/kg
  LD50 (mouse, subcutaneous): 85.7 mg/kg
  Estimated human lethal dose: 1759.5 mg
  Danger level: Lower
