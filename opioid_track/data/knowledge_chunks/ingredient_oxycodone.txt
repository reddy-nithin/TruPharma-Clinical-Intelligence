Oxycodone is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 12.0 nM. Mu opioid receptor agonist. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.
