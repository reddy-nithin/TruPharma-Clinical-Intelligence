PHARMACOLOGY: OXYMORPHONE

Identifiers: RxCUI=7814, ChEMBL=CHEMBL963, PubChem CID=5284604
Molecular: C17H19NO4, MW=301.34

Receptor Binding Affinities:
  - mu: Ki = 1.79 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - kappa: Ki = 25.3 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - delta: Ki = 70.0 nM (source: ChEMBL)

Potency vs morphine: 0.18x

Oxymorphone is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 1.8 nM, showing 14-fold selectivity over kappa. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.
Metabolism: Oxymorphone undergoes extensive hepatic metabolism in humans. After a 10 mg oral dose, 49% was excreted over a five-day period in the urine.
