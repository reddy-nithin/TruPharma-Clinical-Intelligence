PHARMACOLOGY: FENTANYL

Identifiers: RxCUI=4337, ChEMBL=CHEMBL596, PubChem CID=3345
Molecular: C22H28N2O, MW=336.5

Receptor Binding Affinities:
  - mu: Ki = 0.083 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - kappa: Ki = 196.5 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - delta: Ki = 570.0 nM (source: ChEMBL)

Potency vs morphine: 3.85x

Fentanyl is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 0.1 nM, showing 2367-fold selectivity over kappa. Mu opioid receptor agonist. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 8.5 hours
Metabolism: Fentanyl is metabolized to a number of inactive metabolites. Fentanyl is 99% N-dealkylated to norfentanyl by cytochrome P450. It can also be amide hydrolyzed to despropionylfentanyl, or alkyl hydroxylated to hydroxyfentanyl which is N-dealkylated to hydroxynorfentanyl.

  LD50 (rat, unknown): 3.1 mg/kg
