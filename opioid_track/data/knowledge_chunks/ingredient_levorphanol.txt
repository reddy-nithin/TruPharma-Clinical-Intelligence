PHARMACOLOGY: LEVORPHANOL

Identifiers: RxCUI=6378, ChEMBL=CHEMBL592, PubChem CID=5359272
Molecular: C17H23NO, MW=257.37

Receptor Binding Affinities:
  - mu: Ki = 0.21 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - kappa: Ki = 2.3 nM (source: ChEMBL)
  - delta: Ki = 4.2 nM (source: ChEMBL)

Potency vs morphine: 1.52x

Levorphanol is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 0.2 nM, showing 11-fold selectivity over kappa. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 13.5 hours
Metabolism: Levorphanol is extensively metabolized in the liver and is eliminated as the glucuronide metabolite.

Toxicology:
  LD50 (rat, oral): 150.0 mg/kg
  Estimated human lethal dose: 1759.5 mg
  Danger level: Lower
