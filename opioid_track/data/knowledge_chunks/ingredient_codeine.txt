Codeine is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 727.0 nM, showing 35-fold selectivity over kappa. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 3.0 hours
Metabolism: Approximately 70 to 80% of the ingested dose of codeine is metabolized in the liver by conjugation with glucuronic acid to _codeine-6­ glucuronide_ (C6G) and by O-demethylation to _morphine_ (about 5-10%) and N-demethylation to _norcodeine_ (about 10%) respectively.
