Tapentadol is classified as an opioid because it acts as a full agonist at the mu opioid receptor (OPRM1) with Ki = 160.0 nM. Its primary opioid effects — analgesia, euphoria, respiratory depression, and physical dependence — are mediated through mu receptor activation.

Half-life: 4.0 hours
Metabolism: In humans, about 97% of the parent compound is metabolized. Tapentadol is mainly metabolized by Phase II pathways, and only a small amount is metabolized by Phase I oxidative pathways; thus, drug metabolism mediated by cytochrome P450 system is of less importance than phase II conjugation.
